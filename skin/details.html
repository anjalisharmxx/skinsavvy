  • Brightens skin tone

DISEASES TREATED

  • Acne (common in PCOS)
  • Blackheads
  • Whiteheads
  • Keratosis pilaris
  • Skin texture issues caused by hormonal imbalances (common in PCOS)

When to Avoid Salicylic Acid

  • Pregnant women should avoid high concentrations due to potential risks.
  • Diabetic individuals should avoid using it on broken or sensitive skin.
  • Eczema patients should not use salicylic acid, as it can irritate and dry out already sensitive or compromised skin, worsening redness and inflammation.

Advice for PCOS Patients

  • Salicylic acid is safe and effective for managing acne caused by hormonal imbalances related to PCOS.
  • Incorporate it into a balanced routine with ingredients like niacinamide or azelaic acid to help with oil control and pigmentation.

How To Use

  1. Cleanse your face thoroughly.
  2. Apply a small amount of salicylic acid serum or toner to your face using a cotton pad or clean fingers.
  3. Focus on areas prone to acne or congestion.
  4. Follow with a non-comedogenic moisturizer to prevent dryness.
  5. Use sunscreen during the day, as salicylic acid can increase sun sensitivity.

Alternatives for Pregnant Women, Diabetic, Eczema, or Sensitive Individuals

  • Azelaic acid: Safe for all, including pregnant women and those with diabetes, PCOS, or eczema, as it treats acne and reduces inflammation.
  • Niacinamide: Balances sebum production, reduces redness, and is non-irritating.
  • Lactic acid: A gentle exfoliant suitable for sensitive or dry skin types.
  • Sulfur-based products: Effective for acne while being safe for most conditions.
